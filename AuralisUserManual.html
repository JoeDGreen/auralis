1. Introduction

Welcome to Auralis, an accessible application designed for hosting and participating in live voice rooms. Inspired by platforms like Clubhouse, Auralis separates users into "Hosts," "Speakers," and an "Audience."

Auralis operates securely over peer-to-peer WebRTC connections and adheres strictly to WCAG 3 accessibility standards, providing full screen reader support and explicit sound feedback for an effortless navigational experience.

2. Starting Auralis

Auralis comprises two parts: a lightweight background server to connect users, and the primary application interface.

  1. Start the Server: Open the Communicate folder and double-click Server_Manager.pyw. Once the manager window opens, press the Start Server button. Your screen reader will announce that the server is now running.
  2. Launch the App: Leave the Server Manager window open, return to your folder, and double-click Launch_App.bat. This will open the main Auralis application.

Accessibility Note: When managing the server, your focus begins directly on the "Start Server" button, and dynamically shifts to the "Stop" button once the server is actively running.

3. The Lobby

When you start Auralis, you are immediately placed in the Lobby. Here, your microphone is entirely deactivated to ensure complete privacy.

In the Lobby, you are presented with:

  • A text input to type your chosen display name.
  • A dynamic list of "Active Rooms" currently being hosted on the network.
  • A section at the bottom allowing you to start your own room.

4. Hosting a Room

To host a room, navigate to the "Start a New Room" section in the Lobby:

  1. Ensure you have typed your name in the provided text field.
  2. Type your desired room topic or title into the "Room Name" text field.
  3. Press the Create & Host Room button.

Upon creating the room, Auralis will politely request access to your microphone, and your microphone will immediately become LIVE as you are transitioned to the "Stage" as the Host.

Use the **Mute Microphone** button to toggle your audio output at any time. Screen reader logic will confirm whether you are transmitting or muted.

5. Listening in a Room

If you see an active room in the Lobby and wish to listen to the conversation:

  1. Locate the room in the "Active Rooms" list using your Tab key.
  2. Press the Join Room button next to the desired room.

You will join the room strictly as an Audience member. This role is completely passive, meaning Auralis will not even request microphone access. You can relax, listen, and participate safely.

6. Raising Your Hand & Speaking

If you want to contribute to the discussion while in the Audience, you can ask the Host for permission to speak.

  1. Press the Raise Hand to Speak button.
  2. Your status will update to "Hand Raised" and you will hear a chime.
  3. The Host will receive an alert. If the Host approves your request, Auralis will announce "You are now a speaker!" and will seamlessly ask for your microphone permission to place you onto the Stage.

7. Audio Cues (Earcons)

To reduce auditory clutter caused by screen reader chatter, Auralis features specialized sound effects (Earcons) that inform you of system events purely through tone:

  • Happy Ascending Bloop: You successfully joined a room.
  • Sad Descending Bloop: You successfully left a room and returned to the Lobby.
  • Bright Chime: (If you are a Host) An audience member just raised their hand.
  • Tactile Click: You successfully toggled your microphone Mute state.

8. Troubleshooting

  • "Status: Disconnected" on Launch: Ensure that you started the Server using the Server_Manager.pyw application before opening the app. If the app opened before the server started, simply close Auralis and reopen it.
  • Other Users Cannot Join My Room: If testing with multiple friends over the broad internet, verify that your TURN server credentials inside of the renderer.js code block are accurate and supplied by a functioning TURN provider.
